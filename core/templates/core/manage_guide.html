CLUB HANGAR
FLY · LOG · MANAGE

User guide

Club Hangar is a management system for flying clubs — it handles members, bookings, invoicing, aircraft maintenance, and safety from one place. This guide is a process and role reference for admins and instructors, not a button-by-button manual. It covers what the system does for you, what still happens outside it, and how the four main areas connect.

Overview

Club Hangar has two interfaces:

Management web app

Full access for admins and instructors. Members with a role that includes Gantt or Fleet access can also log in here — they see only those areas, not the full management suite. Best on desktop or tablet.

Mobile app (PWA)

For club members. Book flights, view account balance, manage credentials and profile, view notices. Accessed at /app/{{ club.slug }}/ — members add it to their home screen.

The four main areas are people (members, contacts, roles, credentials), bookings (the calendar, flight lifecycle from declaration to completion), finance (account balances, invoicing, charges), and fleet (aircraft, maintenance schedules, airworthiness). They connect: a completed flight generates charges that settle against a member's pre-paid account or produce an invoice; a member's formal standing determines whether they can book; and an aircraft's maintenance state determines whether it can be offered for hire.

Members & contacts

Club Hangar has two different types of person record. Understanding the distinction matters for how you set people up.

Club members

A club member is anyone with a login account in Club Hangar — not just paying members of the club. The term covers:

  • Paying members — ordinary flying members with a subscription, a role, and booking access
  • Instructors — club or contracted flying staff who need accounts to appear on bookings, flight records, and declarations
  • Admins — whoever runs the system (secretary, CFI, club manager)
  • Non-members — trial flight participants, Young Eagles, etc. who need a record but are not club members (standing: Non-member)

Every member record has a standing and optionally a role.

Standing reflects the person's formal membership status within your incorporated society — whether they have been ratified by the committee, are pending that ratification, have let their subscription lapse, have been suspended, or have resigned. This is not just a system flag: under the Incorporated Societies Act 2022, only ratified (Active) members may vote at general meetings or stand for committee, and the society has specific record-keeping obligations for members who resign or are removed. Club Hangar tracks standing to mirror these real-world legal states. The practical system consequences — whether someone can book a flight, access the mobile app, or appear in membership reports — all flow from that underlying status.

Role represents the membership category as defined in your club rules — Full Member, Student Pilot, Life Member, and so on. It carries the annual subscription fee, any special conditions, and optionally grants extra system access. A person's role can change (e.g. a student graduates to full member) without affecting their standing.

"Pending Approval" is a standing, not a role. When a new member appears in the Members list, the Standing column shows "Pending Approval." This tells you their ISA ratification state — the committee hasn't formally voted them in yet. It has nothing to do with their role (e.g. "Full Member" or "Student Pilot"), which is the billing and access category set in Settings → Roles. You can — and usually should — assign a role to a Pending member, because you need one before you can generate their subscription invoice. Standalone, the standing "Pending Approval" just means: account created, not yet ratified.

System access levels

Access level is separate from membership role. A person can be a Full Member (their billing role) AND an Admin (their system access). There are three levels:

Admin

Administrator

Full access: member management, financial records, all settings, every Manage page. Set by ticking "Admin access" on a member record, or by assigning a role with admin flags. There must always be at least one.

Instructor

Instructor

Operational access: Gantt calendar, booking management, member detail pages, invoice list, invoice detail (including marking invoices paid), reports, and safety events. Cannot access the charges screen or settings. Set by assigning a role with the Instructor role flag. Always exempt from booking blocks.

Member

Member (no flag)

Mobile app by default. Role flags can add selective access: Manage (bookings calendar), Fleet (aircraft), Safety (events), Reports (analytics).

Why instructors need a member account: Instructors must appear on booking records, flight completions, and training declarations. Club Hangar finds them, assigns them to bookings, and records their grade-based rate by looking them up as a club member. An instructor who is also a paying club member simply has a role that carries both the instructor flag and a membership subscription — the two are independent. An instructor who is not a club member (e.g. a contracted external CFI) can be given a member account with Pending or Non-member standing, no subscription, and an instructor role.
Instructors and flight charges — what the system actually does

The system does not automatically exempt instructors from hire charges. What it does is:

  • Instructor fee (dual flights) — when an instructor is assigned to a booking, the system calculates an instructor fee from their grade rate and bills it to the booking member (the student), not to the instructor.
  • Aircraft hire — charged to whoever made the booking. If an instructor books an aircraft for their own private hire, they are charged the hire rate like any other member.
  • Booking block — instructors are always exempt, regardless of their account balance.

Whether your instructors pay for personal flying is a club policy decision. The system will generate a hire charge for any booking; it is up to you to zero it out, apply a discount rate, or leave it as-is depending on your arrangement with each instructor. You can delete or adjust charge items manually before applying payment.

Staff instructors who are not club members

Some clubs employ or contract instructors who are not members of the club in the membership sense — they don't pay a subscription, don't have voting rights, and aren't on the ISA register as members. Club Hangar handles this by separating the concepts:

  • Give the instructor a member account with standing set to Non-member or Pending. They get a login, appear on bookings and completions, and can use the management web app.
  • Assign them an instructor role (with the instructor flag). Do not generate a subscription invoice for them.
  • They will not appear in membership counts, ISA register exports, or subscription renewal reports.

Where it gets more complex is if the same person is both an instructor and a full club member — for example, a club CFI who also personally owns shares in a club aircraft and pays a member subscription. In this case, their single account carries both the instructor role and an active standing with subscription tracking. Their instructor grade determines the rate charged to students on dual flights; their membership standing is managed separately through the normal subscription workflow.

Setting up an instructor account
  1. People → Members → + Add manually. Enter their email (and optionally name) and send an invite email so they can set their own password, or set a temporary password yourself by unticking the invite option.
  2. On the Membership tab, assign a role that has the Instructor role flag checked (e.g. a role called "CFI" or "Instructor").
  3. Set Instructor grade if you use grade-based rates (this drives the instructor fee charged to students on dual flights).
  4. Tick On instructor roster — this makes them selectable when assigning instructors to bookings. Untick for instructors who are archived or inactive.
  5. Set standing to reflect their actual membership status: Active if they are a subscribing member, Non-member if they are contracted staff only.
  6. If this instructor also manages the system, tick Admin access separately.

Instructors can log in to the mobile app and the management web app (Bookings calendar and flight detail pages). They do not see Invoices, Members, or Settings.

Training notes

After a completed dual flight, an instructor (or admin) can write a structured debrief note against that flight. Notes are stored permanently on the member's record and are separate from the check-in process — they can be added or updated any time after the flight is completed.

Where to find them: People → Members → [member] → Training tab. The tab lists all notes for that member in reverse chronological order.

Creating a note: Click + New note, select the flight from the dropdown (showing all completed dual flights for that member, most recent first), fill in the three fields, and save. The flight dropdown pre-selects the most recent flight — for a note written immediately after landing this usually needs no change.

  • Exercises covered — what was practised on the lesson
  • Debrief — instructor's assessment of the flight and any points to address
  • Next lesson plan — what to focus on in the next session

Author attribution: The note is attributed to the instructor assigned to the selected flight, not to whoever is logged in and typing. This means an admin entering a note on behalf of an instructor still shows the instructor's name as author — the record reflects who flew, not who entered the data.

Sharing with the member: From the Training tab, each note has two sharing options:

  • Print — opens a print-quality A4 page (same style as invoices) that can be saved as PDF or printed and handed to the member.
  • Email — sends a formatted email directly to the member's email address with all three sections. The member can also see their own training notes in the mobile app under Profile → Training notes.

Contacts

A contact is someone who flies with the club — as a trial flight student, Young Eagles participant, or Gateway Scheme member — but who is not a member of the incorporated society and has no membership subscription. Contacts typically fly in the left seat under instruction from a club instructor; they are students, not passengers. They have no login account. Their name can be added to bookings and flight records, and costs can be attributed to them, but none of the membership lifecycle applies: no standing, no subscription, no ISA register entry.

Organisations (flying schools, companies) can also be added as contacts and marked accordingly.

Converting a contact to a member: If a contact later joins as a member, go to People → Contacts → [contact] → Convert to member. Enter their email address. By default, an invite email is sent so they can set their own password and log in — this is the recommended option. The contact and member records are linked; the contact's booking and flight history remains accessible from both records.

Staff profile

Each staff member (admin or instructor) has a personal profile page accessible from the account menu in the top-right corner of the management web app. From here you can:

  • Contact details — update your name, email, mobile, and address as they appear on your member record
  • Avatar — upload a profile photo, used throughout the app wherever your name appears
  • Notification preferences — choose which push notification types you want to receive. For instructors, this includes options specific to instructor bookings (urgent assignments, upcoming flights). For all users, slot-release notifications can be filtered by aircraft type and instructor, and capped by how far ahead you want to be notified
  • Watched slots — view and remove any bookings you're currently watching (see Slot watch in the Bookings section)

Note: changing your email here changes your login email — inform the member if you're updating someone else's details.

Setting up the club

Club Hangar is configured through two screens: Settings and Settings → Types. Most of this is a one-time exercise, but getting it right at the start avoids problems in invoices, maintenance alerts, and membership records later. The sections below explain each tab, what each setting does, and where its effects surface in the rest of the system.

General

The General tab holds your club's public identity: name, phone, email, and postal address. These are not just display fields — they print verbatim on every invoice the system produces. If your club trades under a different name from its legal entity name, use the trading name here and put the registered legal name in Billing.

The logo appears in the sidebar banner of the management web app. A white-on-transparent PNG at roughly 2:1 aspect ratio works best against the banner colour.

Billing

The Billing tab controls the identity and terms that appear on every invoice the system generates — whether that's a flight invoice, a subscription invoice, or a sundry charge.

Legal name and GST. The legal name should match your entry in the Incorporated Societies Register exactly, since it appears as the issuing entity on all tax invoices. If you are GST-registered, enter your 8–9 digit number and rate. The rate affects invoice tax labelling; your flight and subscription rates are entered separately (Sales → Rates) and should be set inclusive or exclusive of GST consistently.

Bank details and payment terms. The bank name and account number are printed in the invoice footer alongside the payment instructions text — this is what members read when paying by direct credit. The payment terms (days) value sets the due date on every invoice automatically: an invoice raised on 15 July with 30-day terms will show a due date of 14 August. This same value also feeds the booking block — if a member has an overdue invoice older than the configured threshold, they are blocked from requesting new bookings until it is resolved.

Invoice numbering. The prefix (e.g. INV-) and next number together form the invoice number that appears on every invoice sent to members and used for bank reconciliation. The number increments automatically each time an invoice is created. If you are migrating from another system, set the next number here to avoid duplicates with your existing invoice history.

Membership

The Membership tab governs the annual subscription cycle. These settings interact closely with the Billing tab and with individual member records.

Financial year start month. This is the most consequential setting in the system. It defines when each year's subscription period begins and ends, and flows into several places: the "FY27" period label on subscription invoices, the pro-rata amount pre-filled when generating a mid-year invoice, when the auto-lapse check fires, and how the Budget tab's monthly columns are labelled. If your club's subscriptions run 1 April to 31 March, set FY start month to April.

Auto-lapse grace period. After a member's subscription expiry date passes, they are not immediately lapsed. The grace period (days) gives extra time before the system marks them Lapsed — useful if your club traditionally allows a few weeks' leeway at renewal time. Once the grace period passes, members are moved to Lapsed status either overnight by the management command or manually via "Run auto-lapse now" on this tab. Lapsed members can still log in but cannot request bookings.

Registrar's report. At any time you can download a point-in-time register of all Active members as a CSV — suitable for the CAA, an AGM, or an audit. The Members page gives additional date-selection options for producing a historical register as-at a specific date.

Bookings

The Bookings tab controls the Gantt calendar — the core of the club's daily operations.

Operating hours. These set the earliest start and latest start visible on the Gantt. They constrain where new bookings can be created, but do not prevent a flight that departs near closing time from completing after hours. Set these to reflect your normal flying window, not an absolute cutoff.

Booking slots. The slot grid defines the time increments available when creating a booking — every 15 minutes, every 30 minutes, and so on. A finer grid gives more scheduling flexibility but makes the calendar look busier. Use the visual builder to choose which slots are available within the operating window. Slots only affect when a booking can start — they do not limit or snap the booking's duration, and a completed flight is always charged on actual Hobbs/Tacho time regardless of the slot it was booked into.

Booking block. This is the financial gate that prevents members from requesting new bookings when they have unresolved debts. Two independent thresholds can be configured:

  • Unpaid flight charges older than N days — triggered when completed flights have accumulated charges that have not yet been paid or invoiced
  • Unpaid invoices older than N days — triggered when a sent invoice has passed its due date unpaid

Either condition independently triggers the block. The message you configure here is what appears in the mobile app when a blocked member tries to request a booking — make it clear and actionable (e.g. "You have an outstanding balance. Contact the club secretary to resolve it before booking."). Leaving both thresholds blank disables the block regardless of the enable switch. Staff with booking management access are exempt from the booking block regardless of their financial standing.

Appearance

The Appearance tab lets you match the system's colour scheme to your club's identity. The banner colour fills the sidebar header; the primary colour is used for buttons and highlights. Booking status colours (Pending, Confirmed, Departed, Completed, etc.) control how each booking type appears on the Gantt calendar — choosing distinct colours makes it easy to see at a glance how far through the lifecycle each flight is. The body font affects the management web app only; the mobile app uses its own styling. Chart colours affect the bar and line charts in Reports.

Roles

A role is a system-level category that controls what a person in Club Hangar can access and how the billing cycle applies to them. Some roles map naturally to club membership categories (Full Member, Student Pilot, Life Member), but a role is not the same thing as club membership. A person can have a system account — and therefore appear on bookings, flight records, and safety reports — without being a member of the incorporated society. An external CFI, for example, has a system role but is not on the ISA register and pays no subscription.

Three system roles exist by default and cannot be deleted:

  • Admin — all permission flags implied. Full access to every management page including Settings and Types.
  • Instructor — Ops & People + Manage all bookings. See the permission flags below for exactly what this covers.
  • Member — mobile app and Gantt (booking requests only) by default. No management pages.

Custom roles have a configurable permission matrix. Every flag is independent — you can mix and match for roles like "Club Secretary" (Ops & People, no fleet) or "Safety Officer" (Safety only).

Per-person access overrides — on each member's Membership tab, visible only to admins:

  • Config access override CFG↑ — grants Config (Settings) access regardless of role. Useful for a secretary or CFI who needs admin access without a formal Admin role.
  • Superadmin SA — grants the ability to perform destructive operations: delete a member, void a check-in, make manual account adjustments, and run the integrity fix tools. Only a current superadmin can grant or revoke this flag. At least one superadmin must always exist. To grant: open the member's profile → Membership tab → tick Superadmin → Save.

Both badges are visible in the Members list to admins only, so you can see at a glance who holds elevated access.

All members can use the Gantt regardless of role. Any authenticated member can open the booking calendar, see available slots, and submit a booking request. Requests land as Pending and must be confirmed by someone with Ops & People access. Role flags control what happens beyond that.

Bookings access — four levels:

  • None — mobile app only; cannot view or create bookings anywhere.
  • View own — can see their own bookings in the mobile app and Gantt; cannot create or modify.
  • Manage own — can create, edit (while pending), and cancel their own bookings via Gantt and mobile app.
  • Manage all — can create bookings for any member, confirm pending bookings, record departures and completions, and access the booking detail page for all bookings. Combined with Ops & People, this is the full instructor/operational level.

Ops & People — unlocks the main management sections:

  • Operations: Gantt calendar (management view), Bookings page (all bookings list and Attention tab), Block-outs
  • People: Members list, member detail pages (including credentials, account, and financial tabs), Contacts, Invites
  • Sales: Invoices list, invoice detail, mark invoice paid
  • Without this flag, "Manage all" bookings access only reaches the Gantt — not the full booking management or member finance pages.

Fleet — unlocks the Fleet section: aircraft list, individual aircraft detail (settings, rates, maintenance schedule, tech log), and airworthiness status. Does not include block-outs (those are under Ops & People).

Safety — unlocks the Safety section: all submitted occurrence reports, the safety action item register, and the ability to change a report's status or add internal notes. Members can always submit their own occurrence reports from the mobile app; this flag is for those who review and manage them.

Reports — unlocks the Analytics section: utilisation charts, revenue summaries, hours by member, and any exported reports. Read-only; no data is modified.

Config — unlocks Settings and Types. Anyone with this flag is treated as an admin by the system regardless of other flags. Assign only to people who should be able to change billing configuration, roles, flight types, and club-wide settings.

Superadmin — not a role flag. Granted per person via the Superadmin toggle on their Membership tab (see above). Implies all permissions plus access to destructive operations.

Renewal required — members on this role are included in the annual subscription cycle and will be flagged on the Integrity page if their subscription has lapsed. Uncheck for life members, non-member instructors, or anyone whose account exists for operational purposes only and is not subject to annual renewal.

Each role also carries an annual renewal fee. This pre-fills the amount when generating a subscription invoice for anyone on that role. It can be overridden per invoice, but a sensible default saves time at bulk renewal. Roles with no fee set will produce a blank amount field on the invoice modal.

Budget

The Budget tab lets you set monthly hour targets for each aircraft in the current financial year. These budgeted hours appear in Reports as a variance chart — actual hours flown against plan. This is purely a planning and reporting aid; the budget has no effect on bookings, charges, or any other operational function.

Types (Settings → Types)

Settings → Types is a separate screen for the classification lists that label records across the whole system. Each list is configured once and then appears as a dropdown wherever that concept is used — when requesting a booking, completing a flight, logging a maintenance entry, or submitting an occurrence report.

Flight types. The options available when requesting or completing a booking: Solo, Dual, Check Flight, Cross-Country, Navigation Exercise, etc. Each type carries five flags that shape behaviour throughout the system:

  • Solo — hides the instructor field on the booking form. Members who select a solo type cannot attach an instructor to the booking. Note: this flag is for truly unaccompanied, unsupervised solo flights. Supervised student solo flights — where an instructor is present on the ground and remains responsible for the flight — should use a standard dual or training flight type without the Solo flag, so the supervising instructor can be allocated to the booking as normal. The distinction matters for billing (instructor fee) and for the departure gate on the mobile app.
  • Training — tags the flight as a training flight in reports and analytics. No operational effect beyond categorisation.
  • Requires declaration — the flight type requires a departure declaration to be submitted before the aircraft can depart. Use this for cross-country flights, private hire, or any operation where a formal pre-flight record and SAR time is expected. See Departure declarations.
  • Billable — flight time on this type generates charges. Uncheck for positioning flights, ferry flights, or any non-revenue operation you do not want to bill for.
  • Contact — marks the flight type as one intended for non-member contacts (trial flights, Young Eagles, Gateway Scheme, etc.). See Contact flight bookings below for how this works on the Gantt calendar.

Charge types — instructor grades. Define the grades your club uses (C-Cat, B-Cat, A-Cat, Flight Examiner, etc.) along with the hourly rate for each. When an instructor with a given grade is assigned to a booking, their rate determines the instructor fee charged to the booking member. This is set per instructor on their profile (People → Instructors), referencing the grades defined here.

Charge types — aircraft surcharges. Per-flight additions that apply to specific aircraft — a 4-seat aircraft levy, an avionics equipment surcharge, a glass cockpit fee. You define the type and amount here, then assign it to individual aircraft under Fleet → Aircraft. Every flight on that aircraft automatically includes the surcharge as a line item in the charge calculation.

Block-out types. Categories for taking aircraft out of service: Annual inspection, 100-hour check, Maintenance, Paint — whatever your club uses. Aircraft block-outs are always hard: they prevent any booking on that aircraft during the block-out period and appear on the Gantt as a coloured band. All-resource block-outs (e.g. aerodrome closure) can be hard or soft — a soft block-out is a visual advisory only and does not prevent bookings. The colour you assign each type helps staff distinguish maintenance windows from closures at a glance.

Aircraft types. The type list (Cessna 172, Piper PA-28, Robin DR400, etc.) is referenced in two places: each aircraft record has a type, and member credential records reference types for type ratings. The optional ICAO designator is useful for formal reports and occurrence submissions. You cannot add an aircraft to the fleet without first defining its type here.

Event types. The categories available when submitting an event report: Near Miss, Bird Strike, Airspace Infringement, Hazard Observation, etc. These are chosen by the member or staff member at submission and used to filter and analyse events in Reports → Safety. Use the toggle in the Active column to enable or disable a type — inactive types are hidden from the submission form but retained on existing reports. An event type cannot be deleted once it has reports referencing it; deactivate it instead to remove it from the submission form while keeping the historical record intact.

Contact types. Categories for non-member contacts: Trial Flight, Young Eagles, Gateway Scheme, Commercial Hire, etc. When a contact appears on a flight record or payment, the type records why they were flying — useful for reporting on introductory flights or scheme participation.

Maintenance types. Club-level templates for recurring maintenance tasks — 100-hour check, Annual, Oil change, etc. Define a type with optional default recurrence intervals (days and/or hours) and warning thresholds, then select that type when adding a maintenance item to an aircraft. The type pre-fills the intervals and thresholds, so you do not need to re-enter them for every aircraft. The link between a type and an item is a label only — changing a type does not retroactively update items already applied to aircraft; edit individual items if you need to change their values.

Aerodromes (Manage → Aerodromes)

The aerodrome list is separate from Settings — it lives under Manage → Aerodromes and is accessible to admin and instructors. Set it up early: the home aerodrome is used by the Weather screen to pull the METAR, and landing fees are attached to destination aerodromes so they can auto-populate at flight completion.

Adding an aerodrome. Enter the ICAO code (e.g. NZWN), full name, and any notes. Mark one aerodrome as Home — only one can be home at a time; changing it updates the METAR source immediately. Add all aerodromes your members commonly fly to, so they appear in the destination dropdown at completion and their fees are applied automatically.

Landing fee types. Each aerodrome can have multiple fee types — Full Stop, Touch & Go, Night Surcharge, etc. Define these under the aerodrome record with a default amount per operation. At flight completion, when a destination aerodrome is selected, the system presents the applicable fee types for staff to confirm or override. The confirmed fees appear as line items in the charge calculation alongside the aircraft hire and instructor fee.

Active / inactive. Aerodromes can be set inactive if a destination is no longer used — they stop appearing in dropdowns but historical flight records that reference them are preserved.

First-time checklist

Complete these in roughly this order. Several items depend on earlier ones (you cannot configure aircraft rates without aircraft types; you cannot assign grades without instructor grades).

  • ☐ General — club name, contact details, logo
  • ☐ Billing — legal name, GST number and rate, bank account, payment terms (days), payment instructions text, invoice prefix
  • ☐ Membership — FY start month, lapse grace period
  • ☐ Types → Aircraft types — add all aircraft types before adding aircraft
  • ☐ Types → Flight types — solo, dual, check flight, cross-country; set billable flag for each
  • ☐ Types → Instructor grades — grades and hourly rates (e.g. C-Cat $65/hr)
  • ☐ Types → Block-out types — 100-hr, Annual, Maintenance etc. with colours
  • ☐ Roles — create system roles with permission flags, renewal required flag, and annual fees
  • ☐ Aerodromes — home aerodrome and regular destinations (Manage → Aerodromes); landing fees per aerodrome
  • ☐ Aircraft — registration, type, instruments, billing method, maintenance source, initial maintenance hours, maintenance schedule
  • ☐ Rates — aircraft hourly rates per aircraft (Sales → Rates)
  • ☐ Bookings — operating hours, slot grid, booking block thresholds and message
  • ☐ First admin member — ensure at least one person has admin access before you step back

Adding a member manually: Use + Add manually for someone who joined in person or whose email you already have. By default the form sends them an invite email so they can set their own password — the same secure flow as the standard invite. Untick "Send invite email" only if you need to set a specific temporary password yourself (for example, to configure the account before they arrive). When untick is used, leave the password field blank to auto-generate one — note it immediately, as it won't be shown again.

5 — Assign role and send invoice (member still Pending): Member detail → Membership tab → assign the appropriate role if not pre-assigned from the invite (this sets the fee rate). Click Generate subscription invoice. The pro-rata amount pre-fills based on days remaining to FY end. Click Generate & send. The invoice is created and the member receives a push notification — their standing remains Pending at this point.

6 — Member pays: EFTPOS or cash — an instructor or admin marks the invoice paid at point of sale (Sales → Invoices). Bank transfer — the bookkeeper reconciles the payment at month end and marks it paid.

7 — Mark paid → Active + expiry set: When the invoice is marked fully paid, the system automatically sets standing to Active and sets subscription_expires to the date on the invoice. Both changes are recorded in the audit log. The member can now book flights.

ISA 2022: Generating the invoice before committee ratification is fine — standing stays Pending until payment. Do not manually set standing to Active before ratification, as the membership history audit log records every change with timestamp and who made it.

Subscription invoice

On the member's Membership tab, click Generate subscription invoice (next to the Save button):

  • Amount — pre-calculated pro-rata: days remaining to FY end ÷ FY total days × role annual fee. Editable.
  • Subscription valid to — defaults to the FY end date (e.g. 31 March 2027 for FY27). Editable for non-standard cases.
  • Notes — appear on the invoice (e.g. "Pro-rata from June — new member").

Click Generate & send — invoice is created as Sent immediately and the member receives a push notification. For new members (standing Pending), marking the invoice paid will automatically set standing to Active.

Pro-rata, expiry date, and what happens if payment is late

The pro-rata amount is calculated at the moment you open the modal. The expiry date is the FY end — it doesn't shift if payment is delayed. If a member pays 30 days late on a 9-month pro-rata invoice, they get 8.something months of valid membership, not 9. The due date on the invoice (set from your payment terms) is the signal to the member of when payment is expected.

This is intentional: all members on the same role are aligned to the same FY end date, which makes bulk renewal much simpler. A member who delays paying simply loses a few weeks of their subscription window — the same model used by most clubs and many subscription services.

If you want to adjust for a significant delay (e.g., invoice sat unpaid for months), void the original and re-generate with today's date as the start. The new pro-rata will recalculate automatically.

Duplicate invoice protection

The system blocks generating a second subscription invoice if one already exists with status Draft or Sent. The Generate button is hidden and replaced with a link to the outstanding invoice. To re-issue, void the existing invoice first (Sales → Invoices → find it → Void), then generate a new one.

When the invoice is marked paid (Sales → Invoices → Mark paid), the member's subscription expiry date is automatically updated to the date on the invoice, and an audit entry is written to the History tab.

Annual renewal & auto-lapse

The renewal cycle runs once a year, timed to your financial year end (Settings → Membership → FY start month). Here is the full sequence from start to finish — what you do, what happens automatically, and what members experience.

Step 1 — Send renewal invoices (4–6 weeks before FY end)

Go to Settings → Membership → Send renewal invoices. The page shows a preview list of all currently Active members with renewal-required roles who do not already have an outstanding subscription invoice. Check the list, then confirm. The system creates full-year invoices for everyone on the list in one action and sends each member a push notification and email. Members who already have a Sent invoice (from an earlier attempt) are skipped automatically.

The invoice email includes the invoice amount, due date, and your club's bank account details for payment (from Settings → Billing → Bank account). Members pay by direct bank transfer outside the app — there is no in-app payment.

Each invoice is dated today and carries a due date based on your payment terms (Settings → Billing → Payment terms). The expiry date on the invoice is set to FY end, which is the date the system will advance the member's subscription_expires to when you mark it paid.

Early-bird pricing (optional)

If your club offers a discounted renewal fee for members who pay early, configure it in Settings → Membership → Early-bird amount and Early-bird cutoff before sending renewal invoices.

  • Early-bird amount — the discounted fee accepted as payment in full (e.g. $270 instead of the standard $300).
  • Early-bird cutoff — expressed as a number of days before FY end. For example, 60 days with a 31 March FY end gives a cutoff of 31 January. The computed date is shown on the settings page so you can confirm it before sending.

When both fields are set and you run the bulk renewal send, every invoice generated is stamped with the early-bird amount and cutoff date. The invoice (both the emailed PDF and the in-app detail page) shows the discounted rate prominently in green, with the cutoff date and the full amount if paid late. Members do not need to do anything differently — they simply pay; you record whether the payment arrived in time.

When recording a payment: if the invoice has an active early-bird rate, the payment form shows a confirmation checkbox: "I confirm this payment was received on or before [cutoff date]". You must tick this before the form will accept a payment at the discounted amount. This is intentional — it forces the bookkeeper to verify the payment date against the bank statement rather than applying the discount automatically. After the cutoff date passes, the checkbox disappears and the full invoice amount becomes due.

Configure early-bird before sending invoices. The amounts are stamped onto each invoice at the time of generation. If you send invoices before setting up early-bird pricing, those invoices will not carry the discounted rate — you would need to void and regenerate them. Set it up first.

If early-bird pricing is not used, leave both fields blank. The fields have no effect unless both are set.

What happens automatically — expiry warning emails

Separately from the invoice, Club Hangar sends automatic reminder emails to members at 30 days and 7 days before their subscription expiry date. These are sent by the daily notification job and tell the member their subscription expires soon and to contact the club. They fire based on the subscription_expires date already on the member's record — independent of whether an invoice has been sent. Members who have already renewed (expiry date advanced) will not receive these reminders.

Step 2 — When a payment arrives (admin or bookkeeper)

Members pay by bank transfer. The process of matching payments to members happens outside the app — you check your bank statement and reconcile manually. When you confirm a payment has arrived:

  1. Go to Sales → Invoices and find the member's subscription invoice (filter by status: Sent).
  2. Open the invoice and click Mark paid. Enter the amount received and payment date.
  3. The system automatically advances the member's subscription_expires to the date on the invoice (FY end) and writes an audit entry. Their standing remains Active — no manual change needed.

Alternatively, go to People → Members → [member] → Membership tab — a link to any outstanding subscription invoice is shown there, so you can mark it paid from either screen.

Step 3 — After FY end: dealing with non-renewers

Once the FY end has passed and your grace period has elapsed, go to Settings → Membership → Run auto-lapse now. The system checks all Active members with renewal-required roles and moves anyone whose subscription_expires is older than today minus the grace period to Lapsed status. Their login is disabled and they can no longer make bookings. A membership history entry is written for each person lapsed. No email is sent automatically at this point — contact non-renewers however your club normally does (email, phone).

Members on roles with Renewal required unchecked (life members, non-member staff) are never auto-lapsed.

Renewing a single member

For mid-year joiners, role changes, or anyone who missed the bulk send: go to People → Members → [member] → Membership tab → Generate subscription invoice. The modal pre-fills a pro-rata amount based on days remaining to FY end, which you can override. Click Generate & send. When the member pays and you mark the invoice paid, the expiry date advances to FY end and standing is updated.

Lapsed members who want to rejoin

A lapsed member is no longer a member of the incorporated society — their login is disabled and they have no active membership rights. To bring them back:

  1. The committee should formally vote to readmit them (ISA 2022 requirement — same as a new member). Record the meeting reference in your minutes.
  2. Go to People → Members → [member] → Membership tab. Re-enable their login by ticking Active account, then generate a subscription invoice.
  3. When they pay and you mark it paid, the system advances their expiry date. Manually change their standing from Lapsed to Active on the Membership tab and save — the standing change will not happen automatically for lapsed members, only for Pending members paying their first invoice.

Member register (ISA register)

The People → Members → Registrar report (also linked as the Registrar export) produces a point-in-time register of members as at any given date. This is the tool for meeting the Incorporated Societies Act 2022 obligation to maintain an up-to-date register of members.

Enter an "as at" date — defaulting to today — and the system returns all members who had active membership on that date: those who had joined on or before that date and had not yet resigned. The register includes name, email, role, standing, join date, resigned date, date of birth, address, and mobile number.

The report can be viewed on screen as an HTML table, or downloaded as a CSV (Excel-compatible, UTF-8 BOM) for attaching to committee meeting minutes or submitting to your society's Registrar. Bookings

Gantt calendar

The Gantt calendar (Operations → Bookings) is the primary working view for managing the club's flying programme. Each horizontal row represents one aircraft or instructor; the horizontal axis is time across the day. Bookings appear as coloured blocks — the colours correspond to the booking status colours configured in Settings → Appearance. Block-out periods appear as a colour band in the row, labelled with the block-out type.

Navigation. Use the previous/next day arrows at the top to move one day at a time, or click the date to open a date picker.

What you can do depends on your access level. The Gantt is available to all three access levels but behaves differently for each:

  • Admins and instructors — double-click any booking block to open the full booking detail overlay, which shows the member, aircraft, flight type, status, and all available actions (Confirm, Check out, Check in, View charges). Right-click a pending or confirmed booking for a shortcut to Edit details (time, aircraft, instructor, flight type). Right-clicking a departed booking offers Track aircraft live via ADS-B. Admins and instructors can also click any empty aircraft row slot to create a new booking, and drag existing bookings to move or resize them.
  • Members (with Gantt access) — members whose role includes bookings calendar access can also view the Gantt. They see all bookings but can only interact with their own: single-clicking their own pending booking opens an edit form where they can adjust the time, aircraft, or flight type. Clicking their own confirmed booking shows a message to contact the club to make changes. Clicking another member's booking offers a Watch slot option — if that booking is later released (cancelled), they receive an alert so they can take the slot.
  • Members (mobile app) — by default, members without explicit Gantt access use the mobile app schedule view to request bookings. The mobile app does not show other members' bookings or instructor rows.

Creating a booking from the Gantt. Admins and instructors click an empty slot in any aircraft row. The aircraft and start time are pre-filled from where you clicked. Members request bookings from the mobile app or, if they have Gantt access, from the new booking button in the toolbar.

Booking detail. Double-clicking a booking (admins/instructors only) opens the booking detail panel: member name, aircraft, flight type, instructor (if dual), current status, and all available actions for that status. From here you can confirm, check out, check in, or view and record charges.

Row indicators. A small coloured dot appears to the right of each aircraft registration showing its worst outstanding maintenance urgency: green = all items current, amber = a service is due soon, red = an item is overdue. Instructor rows show a similar dot for credential health: green = all credentials current, amber = a credential expiring within 60 days, red = an expired credential.

Credential alerts. If a member on an upcoming booking has an expired or expiring licence, medical, BFR, or type rating, a warning spine appears on the left edge of the booking block. Staff can click through to the credential detail from the booking detail panel.

Attention page

The Attention page (the badge count in the sidebar, or Operations → Attention) collects everything that needs a staff decision before flying proceeds. It is the recommended starting point at the beginning of each day.

Items surfaced on the Attention page include:

  • Maintenance clashes — an upcoming booking is on an aircraft with a Red maintenance item (overdue or past threshold). The booking should be cancelled or rescheduled, and a block-out created while maintenance is carried out.
  • Expired credentials — a member on a confirmed or pending upcoming booking has an expired licence, medical, BFR, or required type rating. The booking cannot safely proceed until the credential is renewed or the booking is amended.
  • Lapsed members with active bookings — a member's standing was moved to Lapsed after their booking was confirmed. They cannot book new flights but existing confirmed bookings are not automatically cancelled — a staff member must decide whether to honour or cancel them.
  • Meter gaps — unexplained gaps in hobbs or tacho readings from recent completions. These affect maintenance hour calculations and need to be resolved (see Hours tracking).
  • Unpaid charges — completed flights where charges have been calculated but not yet collected or invoiced.

The sidebar badge count reflects the total number of open items across all categories. Resolving an item (completing payment, cancelling a booking, updating a credential) removes it from the list.

Block-outs

A block-out marks a resource as unavailable for booking during a defined period. Block-outs can target aircraft, specific instructors, or all resources simultaneously. Each block-out type (configured in Settings → Types → Block-out types) carries a colour for the Gantt, a target resource category, and a hard/soft flag.

Aircraft block-outs are always hard — they prevent any booking on the blocked aircraft in that window and appear as a coloured band in the aircraft's Gantt row. Use them for maintenance windows, 100-hour checks, annuals, or unscheduled downtime. The availability search (Find slots) will not offer blocked aircraft.

Instructor block-outs target a specific instructor and appear on their row in the Gantt. Instructor block-out types can be hard or soft: a hard instructor block prevents bookings being assigned to that instructor in the blocked window; a soft one is advisory — it shows on the calendar but does not prevent assignment. Use hard instructor blocks when an instructor is definitely unavailable (travel, sick leave). Use soft ones for scheduled preferences (e.g. "office admin morning" where they might still be available in an emergency).

Relationship to all-resource block-outs. All-resource block-outs cover every aircraft and instructor simultaneously — for aerodrome closures, club events, or NOTAM periods where no flying should take place. When an all-resource block-out is in effect, you do not need to add separate instructor block-outs for each instructor: the all-resource block-out already prevents assignment across the board. Use an instructor block-out only when a specific instructor is unavailable while the rest of the operation continues normally.

Relationship to the instructor roster. The instructor roster (the On instructor roster flag on a member record) is a higher-level gate than a block-out. An off-roster instructor does not appear in the booking form at all and cannot be assigned to any booking — no block-out is needed to enforce that. Instructor block-outs only make sense for rostered instructors: they say "this person is normally available but not during this specific window." If an instructor should never be assignable (e.g. they have left the club or gone on extended leave), remove them from the roster rather than adding block-outs. See Instructor roster for how to manage roster membership and availability windows.

All-resource block-outs cover every aircraft and instructor simultaneously — for aerodrome closures, club events, or NOTAM periods where no flying should take place. These can be hard or soft depending on how absolute the restriction is.

Creating a block-out. Go to Operations → Block-outs and click + Add block-out, or click an empty row in the Gantt to open the new block-out form pre-filled with the resource and date. Set the scope (which aircraft or instructor, or all), time window, and block-out type. Recurring block-outs can be set as weekly (e.g. every Wednesday afternoon) or daily, with optional active-from / active-until bounding dates.

Existing bookings. Creating a block-out does not automatically cancel bookings already in that window. Clashes appear on the Attention page — you decide whether to cancel the affected booking or move the block-out. This avoids inadvertent cancellations for members who booked in good faith before the block-out was created.

Removing a block-out. Delete from Operations → Block-outs. The blocked slots immediately become available again.

Instructor roster

The instructor roster controls which instructors appear on the Gantt calendar as selectable rows, and which can be assigned to dual bookings. It is managed from People → Instructors in the management web app.

On-roster flag. Each instructor-role member has an On instructor roster checkbox on their member record (People → Members → [instructor] → Membership tab). When ticked, the instructor appears as a named row in the Gantt calendar, can be selected when creating a dual or instruction booking, and is included in the booking assignment check. Untick this to archive an instructor from the active roster without deleting their record — their past bookings and flight history are preserved.

People → Instructors page. This page shows all rostered instructors with a rolling 5-week calendar view. For each instructor you can see which days they are shown as available. From here you can also add a rostered instructor (from among members with an instructor-type role) or remove one from the roster. Adding/removing updates the On roster flag on the member record.

Removing an instructor from the roster. If an instructor is removed from the roster (or has the On roster flag unticked) after they have already been assigned to a booking, the system does not automatically cancel those bookings. The conflict appears on the Attention page under "Instructor off roster" and as a warning on the booking block in the Gantt. The assigned instructor must be changed or the bookings cancelled — they cannot proceed to Depart without resolution or a staff override.

Availability windows

An instructor with no active availability windows is treated as unavailable — they will not appear in Find slots results for dual flights. Every rostered instructor must have at least one active window configured. To reflect a regular schedule, set up availability windows under People → Instructors → [instructor] → Availability tab. Windows can be:

  • Weekly recurring — e.g. every Tuesday all day, every Mon/Wed/Fri 09:00–13:00. The system uses this pattern when members search for available slots. You can set an active-from date (when the pattern starts) and an active-until date (when it ends). Leave active-until blank for an open-ended ongoing pattern.
  • One-off (specific date) — e.g. available this coming Saturday for a check flight, outside their usual pattern.

Availability windows drive the Find slots search for dual flights — a slot is only offered if at least one rostered instructor has a window covering it. They do not hard-block manual booking creation — a booking can still be created and assigned to an off-roster instructor, but the mismatch will appear on the Attention page as a scheduling conflict. For the common case of an instructor who is always available (e.g. a full-time flying school instructor), add a weekly all-day window for each day of the week.

How windows are displayed. Multiple weekly windows with the same time settings are grouped into a single display row — for example, three separate windows for Mon, Wed, and Fri all day appear as one row labelled "Mon, Wed, Fri | All day." This makes the list easy to read without hiding the underlying detail. Each row has its own edit and delete controls that act on all windows in the group.

Active vs past windows

A window is active if it can still apply to a future date:

  • A weekly recurring window is active if its active-until date is blank (ongoing) or has not yet passed.
  • A one-off window is active if the specific date is today or in the future.

Windows that no longer apply to any future date automatically move into the past list and are hidden from the active table. The past list is still accessible via the "Show N past entries" toggle at the bottom of the Availability tab — useful for reviewing your configuration history. Past windows have no effect on availability searching or the Find slots results.

Editing a window

Clicking the edit button on an availability window opens the add form pre-filled with that window's settings. Saving an edit deletes the original window(s) and creates new ones in their place. This is a forward-only operation: it changes the scheduled availability pattern from now on, but it does not alter any bookings that were already made when the old window was in effect. Past bookings are independent records — they do not "check" the availability window list at completion time, only at the point a slot is searched or offered.

Editing is safe for historical records. If an instructor changed from flying every Monday to flying every Tuesday, edit the window freely. Every booking that was made based on the old Monday availability remains untouched — it was a real booking created by a real person, and it lives in the booking table independently of any window settings.

Deleting a past window

Deleting a past window has no effect on historical bookings or the Gantt. Bookings made during the period that window was active are real records — they exist in the bookings table and are not linked to or dependent on the availability window that made the instructor visible at search time. Removing a past window from the Availability tab just tidies the display; it changes nothing in the flight history.

Block-outs layered on top of availability windows

Availability windows and block-outs operate as two separate layers, and both must be satisfied for an instructor to appear available:

  • Availability windows — the baseline pattern of when this instructor is generally available. Set once and updated infrequently.
  • Block-outs — specific periods of unavailability overlaid on top of the baseline. Created for one-off absences (leave, training, illness) without changing the standing availability pattern.

A day that falls within an availability window and within a block-out is treated as unavailable — the block-out takes precedence. This means you can set a standing weekly pattern and then apply individual block-outs for the exceptions, rather than deleting and recreating the window each time something changes. See also Block-outs for the full block-out model.

Instructor block-outs vs editing availability windows. Use a block-out for a temporary absence (holiday, training course) — it suppresses the instructor for a defined period without touching the underlying availability pattern. Use editing the availability window when the instructor's regular schedule has permanently changed — for example, swapping from Tuesday/Thursday to Wednesday/Friday. Don't add recurring block-outs to simulate a permanent change; that creates ongoing clutter to manage.
Worked example — a full year with a schedule change and holiday

Setup (January): Alice is added to the roster. You add a weekly availability window: Mon, Wed, Fri | All day | active from 1 Jan. The window appears as a single grouped row in the Availability tab. The roster calendar shows her initials on every Mon/Wed/Fri for the next five weeks.

Easter block-out (March): Alice is unavailable Easter weekend (Fri–Mon). You add an instructor block-out for those four days. The Find slots search no longer offers Alice on those specific dates, even though a Mon/Fri window covers them. The availability window is untouched — it still shows her as available for all other Mon/Wed/Fri dates.

Schedule change (June): Alice changes her flying days to Tue/Thu. You click Edit on the Mon/Wed/Fri grouped row, change the days to Tue/Thu, and save. The old windows are removed and two new windows (Tue and Thu) are created. Past bookings made on Mondays and Fridays before June are completely unaffected — they are independent booking records. The Attention page does not flag them because the bookings themselves were not changed.

Active-until expiry (December): Alice goes on extended leave. Rather than deleting her windows, you set active-until to her last available day. After that date, the windows automatically move to the past list and she shows as Unavailable on the roster. When she returns, you add a new window with a fresh active-from date.

Viewing history: In the Availability tab, clicking "Show N past entries" reveals the old Mon/Wed/Fri windows (now past) alongside the January–June booking history. The past windows are greyed out and have no effect on current searches.

Instructor block-outs vs availability windows — summary. Availability windows say when an instructor is generally available (used by Find slots and the roster calendar); block-outs say when they are specifically not available (suppresses them for a defined period without changing the pattern). Use availability windows for regular recurring schedules, and instructor block-outs for one-off or temporary unavailability.

Reassigning the instructor

When you open a booking detail and use Change instructor / aircraft, every instructor on the roster is shown with a colour indicator reflecting their availability during that exact time slot:

  • ✓ Available — no other bookings overlap this time slot.
  • ! Pending conflict — has a pending (unconfirmed) booking that overlaps. That booking may still be cancelled, so this is a soft flag rather than a hard block.
  • ✗ Conflict — has a confirmed or in-flight booking that overlaps. Assigning this instructor creates a double-booking that will appear on the Attention page.

All rostered instructors remain selectable regardless of their indicator — the system does not restrict your choice, because staff sometimes need to override or because the conflicting booking may be about to be cancelled. The dots let you make an informed decision and avoid reassigning to an instructor who is already committed.

Rescheduling a booking

From the booking detail panel (click the booking block on the Gantt), staff can reschedule a confirmed or pending booking to a new time. The reschedule form shows available alternative slots for the same aircraft and checks for block-out conflicts before saving. The booking member is notified of the new time via push notification. Use this rather than cancel-and-rebook when the time change is small — it preserves the booking record and audit trail.

Rejecting / cancelling a booking

Either the member (from the mobile app, while the booking is still Pending) or staff (from the booking detail on the Gantt at any status) can cancel a booking. When staff cancel, they can choose whether to release the slot — if released, members watching that booking slot receive a push notification that the time is now free. This is useful when a popular slot opens up and staff want to let waiting members know quickly.

Slot watch

Any staff member (or member from their profile) can "watch" a booking they didn't create. When the booking is subsequently cancelled by staff with the release flag set, all watchers receive a "slot freed" push notification. To watch a booking, click the watch icon on the booking detail panel. To stop watching, unwatch from the same panel or from your Profile → Notifications tab.

This is particularly useful when a member asks whether a slot might come free — instead of having to check back manually, they can set a watch and be notified automatically. Slot watch is opt-in per booking; there is no club-wide "notify me when any slot opens" option (to avoid notification overload).

Correcting mistakes

Most booking status changes can be reversed by an admin, as long as no downstream records have been created that depend on the change. Here is what to do for each scenario:

  • Accidentally confirmed a pending booking — On the booking detail, scroll to the status section and click Undo confirmation. The booking returns to Pending and the member receives an "unconfirmed" notification. Available any time before the flight departs.
  • Accidentally checked out (departed) a booking — On the booking detail, click Undo departure. The booking returns to Confirmed and the departure timestamp is cleared. This only works if no charge items or payments have been added to the flight yet. If charge items exist, you must use Void check-in instead (see below).
  • Accidentally completed a flight (checked in too early or wrong data entered) — On the booking detail, scroll to the charge summary and click Void check-in. This clears all meter readings, charge items, and segments, and returns the flight to Departed so you can redo the check-in correctly. If a payment has already been recorded, you must reverse it first (see below) before voiding. If an invoice has been issued, the void will automatically void the invoice — but only if no payment has been recorded against it. If invoice payment has already been received, the reversal is blocked; contact the member to arrange a credit note or re-issue.
  • Accidentally recorded a payment — On the booking detail, find the payment in the Payments panel and click Reverse. This removes the payment record and reopens the flight for charging. For account credit payments, the reversal refunds the member's account balance; when you re-record payment, the balance is re-debited. Both the original and reversal appear in the member's account ledger as an audit trail. Note: if an invoice has been issued, payment must be reversed from the invoice (Sales → Invoices → [invoice]) rather than from the booking.
  • Cancelled a booking by mistake — Cancellation cannot be undone. Create a new booking for the member at the same time slot.
  • Flight aborted or aircraft fault — club decides not to charge — On the booking detail, scroll to the bottom of the charges section and expand Waive charge (admin only). Enter a reason and confirm. All charge items are removed and the flight is marked completed with zero charge. The waive reason is shown on the charges screen as a permanent note. This cannot be undone — if the decision changes, you must void the check-in and re-complete the flight. Only available when no payment has been recorded.

All reversals are admin-only and are recorded in the booking's audit log (visible at the bottom of the booking detail). If a status button doesn't appear, the prerequisite (no payment recorded, no charge items, etc.) has not been met — the reason is usually clear from context.

Credentials

Club Hangar tracks each member's pilot credentials — licences, ratings, medicals, Flight Reviews (BFR), and certificates. Credential records live on the member record under People → Members → [member] → Credentials tab. Members can also add and manage their own credentials from the mobile app — go to Profile → Credentials.

Credential types. The system uses NZ CAA categories — these are fixed, not club-configurable:

  • Licences — PPL, CPL, ATPL
  • Ratings & endorsements — Cross Country, Night VFR, Instrument Rating (IR), Multi-Engine, Type Rating, Tailwheel, Aerobatic, Seaplane
  • Instructor certificates — C-Cat, B-Cat, A-Cat, Flight Examiner
  • Medical certificates — Class 1, Class 2, Class 3, DLR9
  • Flight Review (BFR) — required every 24 months for PPL/CPL/ATPL holders
  • Other — free-text name for anything not covered above

Adding credentials. Staff can add credentials via People → Members → [member] → Credentials tab → + Add credential. Members can add their own directly from the mobile app under Profile → Credentials → + Add. In both cases: select the type, enter the issue date, expiry date, and certificate number. For Type Ratings, link the record to the aircraft type (from the list in Settings → Types → Aircraft types) — this is how the system knows which aircraft the member is rated for. You can also attach a scanned copy (photo or PDF) of the certificate. A member can have multiple records of the same type — useful for multiple type ratings or overlapping medical renewals.

Expiry monitoring. The system marks a credential as expiring soon when it reaches 60 days from expiry, and expired after the expiry date has passed. Members receive push notifications as credentials approach expiry (configurable in Settings → Notifications). Staff see the same warnings on the Gantt and Attention page.

Where credential warnings appear:

  • Booking block on the Gantt — warning icon on the booking block if the member has an expiring or expired credential relevant to the aircraft type
  • Booking detail panel — credential status listed with expiry dates and current state
  • Attention page — bookings with expired credentials, prompting staff to decide whether to proceed
  • Member detail page — Credentials tab shows the full credential record with expiry status

Credential checks do not hard-block departure. A credential warning does not prevent the Depart action — staff decide whether to proceed and accept responsibility. The correct response is to contact the member, confirm they have renewed (and update the record accordingly), or cancel the booking. Type ratings are checked against the aircraft type on the booking — a member without a type rating for the booked aircraft generates a warning regardless of what other ratings they hold.

Departure declarations

A departure declaration is a pre-flight safety record completed by the pilot before departing. It records: intended route, destination, estimated return time, SAR time, next-of-kin contact, passenger details (with next-of-kin for each), and a series of pre-flight checks (AIP consulted, weather briefed, fuel checked, maps, fuel card, AFM carried, flight plan filed).

Which flights require a declaration? This is configured per flight type in Settings → Types → Flight types → Requires declaration flag. Your club decides which flight types are declaration-obligatory — typically cross-country, private hire, or any flight where a SAR time is operationally important. Dual (instructor) flights do not usually require a departure declaration, because the instructor is present and accountable.

When to complete it. The declaration can be filled in any time before departure — while the booking is Pending, Confirmed, or right before the aircraft departs. Access it from the booking detail: click "Declaration." The member can complete it themselves from the management web app or (for solo flights) be directed to it before departing. Declarations remain in Draft until submitted; only a submitted (non-draft) declaration satisfies the departure gate.

Departure gate and override. If the flight type requires a declaration and none has been submitted, the Depart button will prompt for an override reason rather than silently blocking. Providing a reason allows departure to proceed but records the override and who authorised it — this appears in the booking audit log and on the Attention page. An override should only be used when there's a good operational reason (e.g. member already filed a flight plan externally and the system declaration is duplicative).

Authorising instructor

The declaration includes an optional Authorising instructor field — the instructor who verbally cleared the flight. This is a record-only field: the system does not send an approval request to the named instructor and does not require their confirmation in the system. It simply records who the pilot says authorised the flight.

The key use case is an early-morning private hire departure before club operating hours. The pilot and instructor agree on the flight the day before; the pilot completes and submits the declaration in advance, naming the instructor in the Authorising instructor field. On the morning of the flight, with no instructor physically on site, the pilot departs themselves from the mobile app. The submitted declaration satisfies the departure gate. If the declaration was completed more than 6 hours earlier, the system prompts the pilot to acknowledge that they have re-checked weather and NOTAMs before self-departing — this acknowledgement is recorded in the declaration. The flight proceeds entirely within the system with a full audit trail: who flew, who authorised, when the declaration was submitted, and when the staleness check was confirmed.

Passengers on declarations

Passengers are recorded on the departure declaration, not directly on the flight completion. Each passenger entry can include next-of-kin name and phone. If the same person regularly flies with the club, their details can be saved as a frequent passenger and auto-populated on future declarations for that member — reducing data entry and ensuring completeness.

Contact flight bookings (trial flights, Young Eagles, etc.)

Some flights are flown with non-member contacts — trial flight participants, Young Eagles, Gateway Scheme members — in the left seat under instruction from a club instructor. These are instructed lessons, not scenic rides: the contact is the student pilot, with the instructor alongside. They are recorded as contact flight bookings and work differently from standard member bookings because no membership record exists for the person being invoiced.

Setup: mark the flight type as Contact. In Settings → Types → Flight types, enable the Contact toggle on any flight type that applies to non-member flights (e.g. "Trial Flight", "Young Eagles"). This is a one-time configuration. Once set, those types are treated differently in the booking form.

Only instructors can book contact flights. The Contact flight types only appear in the flight type dropdown for users with an instructor role. Regular members see only non-contact flight types, so they cannot accidentally book as a contact flight.

How to book a trial flight (instructor workflow):

  1. Open the Gantt calendar and click an available slot for the aircraft and time you want.
  2. In the Flight type dropdown, select a Contact type (e.g. "Trial Flight") — these are shown under a Contact flights heading, separate from regular Member flights.
  3. The Member field automatically fills with your own name as the pilot in command (instructor). You can change this if another instructor is flying.
  4. A Contact field appears — search for and select the trial flight participant from your Contacts list. If they are not yet in the system, add them to Contacts first (People → Contacts → + Add).
  5. The Billed to field appears once a contact is selected — choose whether the charge goes to the contact's account or another party (e.g. a Young Eagles sponsoring organisation).
  6. Complete and save the booking as normal.

Switching back to a member flight: If you select a non-contact flight type after having chosen a contact type, the Contact and Billed to fields are cleared and hidden automatically. The instructor field also reappears for non-solo types.

Contacts list. Contacts are managed under People → Contacts. Add a contact record before booking their flight, so they can be searched and attached to the booking. Each contact can have a type (e.g. "Trial Flight", "Young Eagles") and their flight history is recorded separately from member flight history.

Availability search (Find slots)

The availability search (Operations → Find slots, or the Search tab in the mobile app) lets you — or a member — quickly find available time windows without scrolling through the Gantt day by day. Enter an aircraft type, desired duration, and a date range; the system returns a list of windows where a matching aircraft is free, not blocked out, and within operating hours.

This is particularly useful at the start of a busy period when members are trying to book cross-country flights requiring several hours, or when staff are trying to find a gap for a maintenance slot. From the results, clicking a window pre-fills the booking request form.

Flight completion

After landing, complete the flight record:

  • Meter readings — hobbs end / tacho end. The system checks for gaps against the previous flight on that aircraft; unexplained gaps are flagged on the Integrity page (Manage → Integrity).
  • Outcome — completed, training, check flight, etc.
  • Destination aerodrome
  • Passengers — non-member passenger names (required for the flight record)
  • Training declaration — for dual flights, the instructor signs off learning objectives
Meter gaps — what they mean and how to handle them

When you enter a hobbs/tacho start reading that doesn't match the previous flight's end reading on that aircraft, the system asks for a gap explanation. This is not an error — gaps are legitimate (ground run, maintenance, ferry flight by another pilot). You must record a note explaining the discrepancy.

If a reading was entered incorrectly, fix it in Fleet → Aircraft → [aircraft] → Maintenance → View full log →. Find the entry and click the pencil icon to amend the reading, entering a reason. The Integrity page updates immediately. Charges are not recomputed. If the gap represents genuinely unaccounted time (ground run, maintenance tow) and the readings are correct, no amendment is needed.

Accumulating unexplained meter gaps makes your maintenance hours calculations unreliable, since maintenance hours are derived from the same meter readings.

After completing the flight record, review the calculated charges. Charges come from your configured rates (aircraft hourly rate × hours, instructor rate if dual, surcharges, landing fees). You can add, edit, or remove line items before applying.

Outstanding amounts from previous flights

When a member has other completed flights with unsettled charges, the charges screen shows them in one of two sections depending on whether an invoice exists:

Outstanding invoices (informational — no checkbox)

Flights that already have an active invoice (sent or overdue) appear here for information only. They cannot be bundled into the current payment or invoice — each must be paid by recording payment against its own invoice in Sales → Invoices. The balance due and a link to the invoice are shown. Flights with a current, not-yet-overdue invoice that may be awaiting bank transfer reconciliation are hidden entirely — they reappear once overdue.

Outstanding charges (checkboxes — can be bundled)

Flights with no active invoice appear as pre-ticked checkboxes. Tick the ones you want to collect in this session; untick any you want to leave for later. The grand total updates as you tick. These flights can be settled alongside the current flight using any direct payment method, or included in the new invoice if you are issuing one.

Account deficit (checkbox — can be bundled)

If the member's account balance is negative (credit overdraft), an Account deficit (credit overdraft) row appears. Ticking it adds the deficit to the session total; when payment is recorded, the account is restored to zero. This is separate from flight charges — it clears the running negative balance caused by previous credit payments that overdrew the account.

If a payment that included outstanding flights or an account deficit is later reversed, all collected amounts are restored and the rows reappear on the charges screen.

Once an invoice is issued, all payment must be recorded against the invoice — not the booking. After a flight invoice has been raised (any status except Void), the payment panel on the booking detail is replaced with a notice directing you to the invoice. The only way to record payment is through Sales → Invoices → [invoice] → Record payment. This applies regardless of how the member pays — EFTPOS, cash, or bank transfer — because the invoice is the formal billing document and all receipts must be reconciled against it. If you need to revert to direct payment on the booking (for example, because the invoice was created in error), void the invoice first — voiding restores the normal payment form on the booking.

Split flights — multiple pilots or shared costs

A single flight booking can be split between two or more members. Two splitting scenarios exist:

Scenario A — Two pilots fly in sequence (e.g. pilot A then pilot B on the same aircraft)

Use split flight during check-in. Each segment records a pilot and a contiguous block of meter readings; the hours per segment come from the meter delta.

  1. On the check-in screen, tick Split flight / add another person.
  2. Enter the handover meter reading — the point where pilot A handed over to pilot B. This must be strictly between the flight start and end readings.
  3. Select the second pilot from the member list. Add more handover rows for three or more pilots.
  4. Submit check-in. The system validates that each handover reading falls within the overall start → end range and rejects the form if not.
  5. On the payment screen, a payment form appears for each pilot showing their segment charge. Choose a payment method and amount for each and submit them one at a time — each submission allocates a pending payment for that pilot.
  6. Once allocated, each pilot's row shows a Record payment button. Click it to confirm the payment has been collected.

Different payment methods are fine per pilot (e.g. pilot A pays by account credit, pilot B by invoice). The completion's overall payment method shows as "Split" when multiple methods are used.

Scenario B — Two members share the cost of a single flight (e.g. two members share a hire)

Use split payment. The flight is completed as a single block of hours, but the cost is divided between two members.

  1. Complete the flight normally (single set of meter readings).
  2. On the charge screen, use the split payment option to allocate portions of the total to each member.
  3. Each member's portion can use a different payment method.

Both members' accounts or invoices are updated independently. The completion record links to both FlightPayment rows.

Instructor fees vs instructor charges: The instructor fee charge item (from the instructor's grade rate) is billed to the booking member — the student pays for the instruction. A separate question is whether the instructor themselves pays for aircraft hire if they book for personal flying — that depends on your club's arrangement and is controlled by adjusting or deleting the hire charge item before applying payment. See Instructors and flight charges.

Adding a charge after payment has been recorded

Once a flight is fully paid, the add-charge forms are hidden. If a charge was missed — for example, a landing fee not recorded at the time — an admin can reverse a payment to reopen the flight:

  1. On the booking detail, scroll to the payment panel and click Reverse on one of the recorded payment rows.
  2. The payment is un-marked. The flight shows as unpaid and the add-charge forms reappear.
  3. Add the missed charge. The flight total increases accordingly.
  4. Adjust the allocation amounts if needed (e.g. for a split flight), then click Record payment.

For payments originally made by account credit, the reversal temporarily refunds the member's account; the re-record re-debits it. Both transactions appear in the member's account ledger as a paired audit trail. If the original payment also included outstanding amounts from previous unpaid flights, that amount is restored to the outstanding balance as well — the checkbox reappears on the charges screen so it can be re-collected when you re-record payment.

What happens if a flight charge isn't applied?

A completed flight is considered uncharged if no direct payment has been applied and no invoice has been raised — the booking shows as Returned. This can happen if staff complete the flight record but don't get to the charging step — for example, at end of day when there are multiple arrivals, or when the charges need review before billing. Once an invoice is issued or direct payment is recorded, the booking moves to Completed and is no longer uncharged.

Uncharged flights are not lost — they remain on the member's flight record and on the Charges screen. Staff can return and apply charges at any time. The charge amounts remain those calculated at completion time (aircraft rate × hours). If rates have been updated since, the charge items already on the record reflect the rate that was current when the flight was completed.

The booking block has an unpaid flight charges threshold (configured in Settings → Bookings). If the number of days since the flight's completion exceeds this threshold and the charges are still unsettled (no direct payment and no invoice issued), the member's booking block is triggered — they will be unable to request new bookings from the mobile app until the outstanding charges are settled. This is the system's way of flagging flights that have fallen through the cracks.

To check for uncharged flights across all members, use the Attention page — it surfaces completed bookings with no payment applied that are older than your configured threshold. You can also spot them in a member's detail (Account tab) where uncharged completed flights appear in the transaction history without a payment row.

No automatic invoicing. The system never automatically creates invoices. Every invoice is generated by a staff action — either by selecting Invoice as the payment method at the charging screen, or by creating a manual invoice under Sales → Invoices. If staff complete a flight without choosing a payment method, no invoice exists and the member receives no billing notification. The club must monitor the Attention page or Charges screen regularly to catch flights that need billing.

Sales & invoicing

Account balance

Each member has a pre-paid credit account. Members top up by bank transfer (outside the app); charges are deducted as flights are completed.

  • Add credit: Member detail → Account tab → + Add credit. Enter amount and reference (e.g. "Bank transfer ref 12345").
  • The balance can go negative up to a per-member credit limit (set on the Account tab). Below the limit, the booking block triggers.
  • All transactions are visible to the member on their mobile profile.

Invoices

Club Hangar has two completely separate invoicing streams that happen to share the same invoice list — it is important to understand that they are unrelated to each other:

  • Subscription (renewal) invoices — issued once a year per member, for the annual membership fee. Created manually from the member's Membership tab, or in bulk via Settings → Membership. Marking one paid has a special side-effect: the system automatically advances that member's subscription expiry date to the date on the invoice. This is the only way subscription expiry moves forward.
  • Flight invoices — issued for individual completed flights. Generating a flight invoice immediately marks the booking as Completed. The invoice amount is the outstanding balance on the flight (total charge minus any prior direct payments). A $0 invoice is valid when the member has already paid in full by other means and you want to issue a formal receipt. Flight invoices have no effect on subscription expiry.

Sundry invoices are a third type — ad-hoc billing for items like exam fees or training materials — and also have no connection to subscription renewal.

Recording a payment against an invoice

To record a payment: Sales → Invoices → [invoice] → Record payment received. You will see:

  • Method — how the money was received: Cash, EFTPOS, Credit card, Bank transfer, or Account credit. Required.
  • Amount — defaults to the outstanding balance. Change it for a partial payment.
  • Reference — optional. Use this for the bank transaction reference, receipt number, or any note that helps with reconciliation.

Each payment creates an entry in the invoice's payment ledger. The ledger is shown on the invoice detail page and on the printed invoice. The invoice remains as Issued until the total payments equal or exceed the invoice total, at which point it is automatically marked Paid.

Account credit — selecting this method deducts the payment amount from the member's in-system account balance immediately. The debit appears in their account transaction history. Use this when a member asks to settle an outstanding invoice using credit they have topped up into their account.

Bank transfer — bank transfer payments are not automatically detected; they must be entered manually by the bookkeeper after reconciling the bank statement against outstanding invoices. This means bank transfer entries in the payment ledger may lag several days or weeks behind the actual transfer. Members should be made aware that their payment may not appear on the invoice immediately if they paid by bank transfer.

Partial payments

You can record as many payments as needed against a single issued invoice — each instalment creates its own ledger entry showing the date, method, amount, and reference. The invoice stays as Issued after each partial payment and shows the remaining outstanding balance. Only when the cumulative total equals or exceeds the invoice total is the invoice automatically marked Paid.

This is useful when a member pays in instalments, when a bank transfer is received in parts, or when one portion is paid by bank transfer and the rest settled from their account credit.

The payment ledger is printed on the invoice PDF — each instalment appears as a separate row so the member can see exactly what has been received and when.

Proof-of-payment receipts

If a member paid by EFTPOS, cash, or account credit at the time of the flight, no invoice is issued automatically. If they later need written proof of payment — for example, for an employer reimbursement claim — staff can issue one from the booking detail: Invoice instead of direct payment? → Issue invoice.

Because payment has already been recorded, the invoice is created in Paid status immediately. It will not appear as an outstanding item. The printed invoice shows the full charge, the amount paid, Total Due: $0.00, and the date and time payment was made. On screen the invoice shows "✓ Paid in full — [date at time]"; the print/PDF view adds a PAID watermark across the document.

For split flights or shared costs, pressing Issue invoices creates one receipt per payee in a single action — each addressed to that person for their share only.

Proof-of-payment receipts appear under Sales → Invoices filtered by Paid status. They are created already closed and do not pass through the Draft → Sent workflow.

Printing or emailing an invoice

Every invoice has a print view: Sales → Invoices → [invoice] → Print. This renders the invoice as a clean, printable page with the club's billing details, member name, line items, GST breakdown, payment instructions, and bank account number. Use this to print for post, or save as PDF (using your browser's Print → Save as PDF) and attach to an email. There is no automatic email-sending from Club Hangar — sending the invoice to the member is done manually via email or post.

Correcting an invoice (void and re-issue)

Once an invoice is Sent, you cannot edit it — this protects the financial record. To correct it:

  1. Sales → Invoices → find the invoice → Void.
  2. Create a new invoice with the correct amounts.
  3. Send the new invoice to the member.

Voided invoices are retained in the system for audit purposes. They have no financial effect.

Sundry sales (Sales → Sundry)

A sundry sale is a quick debit to a member's account balance for a non-flight transaction — logbooks, aeronautical charts, headsets, exam fees, club merchandise. No formal invoice is generated: the charge goes directly into the member's account ledger as a debit, reducing their balance.

Enter the member, a description, amount, and an optional reference. The transaction appears immediately in the member's account history. Use sundry sales for small point-of-sale items where the overhead of creating and marking paid a full invoice isn't warranted. For larger items or anything that requires a formal tax invoice, use Sales → Invoices → Create instead.

The Sundry page also shows a searchable history of all past sundry transactions, filterable by member name or description.

Vouchers (Sales → Vouchers)

Vouchers are pre-created credit codes that can be redeemed against a member's account balance. Typical uses: trial flight vouchers sold to the public, prize vouchers awarded at airshows, gift vouchers for members.

Creating a voucher. Go to Sales → Vouchers → + Create voucher. Enter a code (e.g. GIFT-2025-001), the dollar value, and an optional description. The voucher is immediately listed as pending.

Redeeming a voucher. When a voucher holder presents their code, go to Sales → Vouchers → find the code → Redeem → select the member. The voucher value is credited to that member's account balance as a top-up transaction. The voucher is then marked as redeemed and cannot be used again. The member's account balance increases immediately, so they can book flights against the credit.

Vouchers cannot be partially redeemed — the full face value is credited on redemption. The pending and redeemed voucher lists can be filtered to show only one category at a time.

Rates

There is no single "Rates" management screen — rates are configured in several places depending on type:

  • Aircraft hire rates — configured per aircraft on the aircraft detail page (Fleet → Aircraft → [aircraft] → Rates tab). The Rates tab shows a default rate field and a grid with one row per flight type.
    • Simple (flat rate): set the default rate and leave all grid rows blank. Every flight on that aircraft is charged at the default rate regardless of flight type.
    • Per-flight-type rates: enter a rate in specific grid rows to override the default for those flight types. Rows left blank still use the default. Use "Fill blanks with default" to pre-populate all blank rows if you want to start from the default and adjust from there.
    • Billing instrument: the aircraft has a primary instrument (Hobbs, Tacho, or Air Switch) set on the Instruments tab. Each grid row can optionally override this — for example, a "Ground run" flight type billed on Tacho while all other types use Hobbs. Rows left with the default use the aircraft's primary instrument.
    The correct rate and instrument are applied automatically at check-in based on the booking's flight type. If no rate is configured for the booking's flight type (and no default is set), no hire charge is generated.
  • Instructor grade rates — configured in Settings → Types → Instructor grades. The grade (C-Cat, B-Cat, etc.) carries the hourly rate. Each instructor is assigned a grade on their member record; the grade rate is snapshotted into the flight completion at the time of check-in, so a rate change mid-year does not affect completed flights.
  • Aircraft surcharges — configured in Settings → Types → Charge types → Surcharge types, then assigned to specific aircraft in Fleet → Aircraft → [aircraft] → Surcharges tab. Each surcharge must be explicitly toggled Active per aircraft — assigning the type alone is not enough. Surcharges can be per flight (a flat amount added once per flight regardless of duration, e.g. a twin-engine levy or a maintenance fund contribution) or per hour (the amount multiplied by flight hours, e.g. an engine reserve levy). For split flights, per-hour surcharges are billed to each pilot based on their own hours; per-flight surcharges are split proportionally. Use the fuel charge rate (below) for fuel-related charges — surcharges are better suited to fixed or slowly-changing levies that do not need a dated rate history.
  • Landing fees — configured per aerodrome under Manage → Aerodromes (see Aerodromes setup). Applied per destination aerodrome at flight completion.
  • Fuel charge rate — a dated per-hour rate set on each aircraft record (Fleet → Aircraft → [aircraft] → Rates tab). When active, it is added automatically at check-in regardless of whether the hire rate is wet or dry. The rate is snapshotted at departure time, so a fuel price change mid-flight does not alter the charge. Add a new entry whenever the rate changes; the most recent effective date applies. You control whether it fires via the Active toggle on the rate — pause it to suspend fuel charging without deleting the history. Use this for direct fuel cost recovery (dry hire clubs) or as a variable market fuel levy on top of a wet hire rate.

When a rate needs to change — for example, before running the annual bulk renewal or changing the C-Cat instructor rate — update it in the relevant place before the affected flights or invoices are generated. Changes take effect for future transactions only; past completions retain their snapshotted rates.

Payment reconciliation (outside Club Hangar)

Club Hangar does not process payments directly — there is no payment gateway integration. The reconciliation process is:

  1. Member pays by bank transfer, using the invoice number as the reference.
  2. Bookkeeper reviews the bank statement and matches each transfer to an invoice.
  3. Bookkeeper notifies admin of received payments.
  4. Admin records each payment in Club Hangar: Sales → Invoices → [invoice] → Mark paid.
  5. For subscription invoices: marking paid automatically sets the member's subscription expiry date.
Why it works this way: Most small clubs use a single club bank account managed by a treasurer or bookkeeper. Keeping payment processing outside the app avoids PCI compliance issues and lets the bookkeeper use their existing accounting software (Xero, MYOB) for the ledger while Club Hangar tracks the membership and operational side.

Aircraft & maintenance

Aircraft setup

Fleet → Aircraft → + Add aircraft. Key fields:

  • Registration and type (links to the aircraft type list for rates and type ratings)
  • Instruments — which meters the aircraft has: Hobbs, Tacho, and/or Air Switch. Each can be enabled independently. An aircraft might have all three, or only one.
  • Billing method — which instrument drives the flight charge calculation. If set to Hobbs, the hire fee = Hobbs hours flown × hourly rate.
  • Maintenance source — which instrument accumulates maintenance hours. This can differ from billing: you might bill members on Hobbs time but run maintenance intervals on Tacho because your maintenance manual requires it.
  • Maintenance fraction — a multiplier applied to the raw maintenance reading. If your tacho runs at 0.83 hours per Hobbs hour (common on fixed-pitch propeller aircraft), set this to 0.83 so that maintenance hours are correctly scaled rather than over- or under-counted.
  • Initial maintenance hours — the accumulated hours already on the aircraft when it was entered into Club Hangar. The system adds all subsequent flight hours on top of this figure when tracking due-hours for maintenance items.
  • Status — Online (available for hire), Offline (in maintenance, bookings blocked), Retired (permanently removed from service)
  • Available for hire — uncheck to hide the aircraft from the booking calendar without changing its status
Instruments, billing, and maintenance are connected. The billing method determines what members are charged; the maintenance source and fraction determine when maintenance items fall due. At every flight completion, staff enter readings for all enabled instruments. The system uses the billing instrument reading to calculate the hire charge, and the maintenance instrument reading to advance the aircraft's accumulated hours — which in turn determines when each maintenance item triggers an alert. Entering readings accurately at completion is therefore not just a billing step; it keeps your maintenance records current. See Hours tracking.
Initial hours matter. Get the initial maintenance hours right when you first add an aircraft, or your due-hour calculations will be wrong from day one. Check the aircraft's last maintenance release for the meter reading at the last service.

Maintenance items

The maintenance items list gives staff advance notice of when scheduled work is coming due, so flights can be planned around it and the aircraft can be made available to the engineer at the right time.

Open Fleet → Aircraft → [aircraft] and select the Maintenance tab. The tab is laid out in three sections: Block-outs (any active maintenance block-outs for the aircraft — check this first before scheduling maintenance), a Current meters strip showing the highest recorded hobbs/tacho/airswitch readings from the maintenance log alongside the total accumulated maintenance hours, and then the Maintenance items list and log. Verify the current meter readings against the physical aircraft at your next inspection — discrepancies indicate a gap in the log.

Each aircraft has a list of recurring maintenance items — tasks that come due on a schedule. Common examples: 100-hour check, oil change, annual inspection, pitot-static check, ELT battery. Each item is configured independently.

An item can have either or both of two scheduling methods:

MethodFieldsHow it works
Hours-based Due hours, Interval hours Item is due when accumulated maintenance hours reach the due hours value. When marked done, the system sets due hours = current hours + interval automatically. E.g. next 100-hour check at 1,234.5h.
Calendar-based Due date, Interval days Item is due on a fixed date. When marked done, the system advances the due date by the interval automatically. E.g. annual inspection due 15 Oct 2026, interval 365 days → next due 15 Oct 2027.

When both are set, whichever limit is reached first triggers the alert. This is the standard approach for items like the 100-hour / annual inspection (whichever comes first).

Setting up a 100-hour / annual (whichever first)

Create one maintenance item called "100-hour / Annual inspection" with:

  • Due hours = current maintenance hours + 100 (e.g., 1,234.5 if aircraft is now at 1,134.5h)
  • Interval hours = 100
  • Due date = date of next annual (e.g., 15 Oct 2026)
  • Interval days = 365

When either limit is reached, the urgency turns Amber then Red. After completing the inspection, click the ✓ Mark done button — the system resets both the due hours (current + 100) and the due date (today + 365) automatically.

Alert thresholds — per item and global defaults

Each maintenance item has optional per-item alert thresholds. If left blank, the global defaults from Settings → Billing are used:

  • Warn hours (amber) — turns amber when this many maintenance hours remain before due. Default: 20 hours.
  • Alert hours (red) — turns red when this few maintenance hours remain. Default: 5 hours.
  • Warn days (amber) — turns amber when this many days remain before due date. Default: 30 days.
  • Alert days (red) — turns red when this few days remain. Default: 14 days.

Set tighter thresholds on items that need more lead time to organise (e.g. a pitot-static recheck that requires booking an avionics shop weeks in advance).

How maintenance hours are calculated

Every time a flight completion is entered, the system calculates the maintenance hours flown and adds them to the aircraft's accumulated total:

  1. Read the end and start values from the configured maintenance source instrument (hobbs, tacho, or air switch).
  2. Multiply the delta by the maintenance fraction (usually 1.00 unless your tachometer reads differently from calendar hours).
  3. Add to the aircraft's running maintenance hours total (starting from initial maintenance hours).
  4. Compare against all maintenance items' due hours and recalculate urgency.

This means maintenance hours are only as accurate as your meter readings. Unexplained meter gaps (entries where start ≠ previous end) introduce error — resolve them promptly via Manage → Integrity.

Alerts and how they surface

Maintenance urgency appears in three places:

LocationWhat you see
Fleet → Aircraft listUrgency indicator (Green / Amber / Red) beside each aircraft
Aircraft detail → Maintenance tabFull list of items with hours/date remaining and urgency per item
Operations → AttentionAny aircraft with a Red maintenance item appears here — review daily

A Red maintenance item does not automatically prevent booking — it is an alert, not a lock. To prevent booking during maintenance, create a block-out (Operations → Block-outs) for the aircraft covering the maintenance period. The block-out prevents all bookings and shows on the Gantt calendar.

Red = maintenance item is due or overdue. The item's due date or hours threshold has been reached and the work has not been recorded as done. The system surfaces this as an alert — it does not automatically prevent bookings. If the aircraft should be grounded, set its status to Offline or add a block-out.

Logging completed maintenance work

When maintenance is done, record it in Fleet → Aircraft → [aircraft] → Maintenance → Log entry:

  • Maintenance item — link to the scheduled item being completed (or free-text for unscheduled work)
  • Date completed
  • Meter reading at completion — hobbs or tacho reading at the time of the service
  • Description of work — what was done, by whom, reference to maintenance release
  • Next due — update the maintenance item's due hours and/or due date to reset the schedule
Unscheduled work — snags and ad-hoc maintenance

Not all maintenance follows the recurring schedule. For unscheduled work triggered by a snag:

  1. Log the snag against the aircraft (from the flight completion record or directly from Fleet → Aircraft → Snags).
  2. If the snag grounds the aircraft, create a block-out to prevent bookings.
  3. Assign an action item if follow-up is required. Open action items appear on Safety → Action items and are counted in the sidebar Attention badge.
  4. When work is complete, log a maintenance entry against the snag, resolve the action item, and remove the block-out.
Meter integrity and maintenance accuracy

Maintenance hours accumulate from hobbs/tacho readings entered on flight completions. Manage → Integrity runs a cross-check across all completions on both Hobbs and Tacho and flags:

  • Gap — next flight's start reading is higher than the previous flight's end reading (unaccounted time)
  • Overlap — next flight's start reading is lower than the previous flight's end reading (a reading was entered incorrectly)

To correct a wrong reading: go to Fleet → Aircraft → [aircraft] → Maintenance → View full log →. Find the relevant entry and click the pencil icon to amend the reading. Enter the corrected value and a reason. The correction cascades to the flight completion record and the Integrity check will immediately reflect the change. Charges are not recomputed when readings are amended.

Unresolved overlaps mean one reading is factually wrong and maintenance hours are being mis-stated. For airworthiness purposes, this is a significant risk.

Weather

The Weather screen (accessible from the sidebar) shows the current METAR and TAF for your club's home aerodrome. It includes wind speed and direction, visibility, ceiling, temperature, dew point, and QNH. Data is fetched via the AVWX REST API (avwx.rest) using the ICAO code of your home aerodrome (set under Manage → Aerodromes) and is cached for up to five minutes.

Not for operational use. Weather data shown in Club Hangar is provided for general situational awareness only. It is sourced from a third-party API and may be delayed, incomplete, or unavailable. It must not be used as the sole source for flight planning, pre-flight weather assessment, or any operational decision. Pilots must obtain weather information from official sources — MetService, NAIPS, AviationWeather.gov, or equivalent — before every flight.

Live map / ADS-B

The Live map (Map, in the sidebar) shows real-time positions of aircraft in flight using ADS-B data. For an aircraft to appear on the map, its ADS-B hex code (ICAO 24-bit address, six hex digits) must be entered on its aircraft record in Fleet → Aircraft. Without the hex code, the aircraft will not be visible on the map even if it is airborne and transmitting.

The map is useful for monitoring aircraft that are on cross-country flights or overdue from a local training area. It is a live view — positions update automatically without refreshing the page. Aircraft that have landed and are stationary may continue to appear briefly before their transponder is switched off.

Note that ADS-B reception is dependent on ground station coverage. Aircraft at low altitude or in terrain-shadowed areas may not be visible even when airborne and equipped with a transponder.

Announcements

Announcements (Operations → Announcements) allow admin to post messages that appear on the mobile app home screen. Use these for operational notices — runway closures, club meetings, temporary airspace restrictions, weather warnings, or any time-sensitive information that all members should see when they open the app.

An announcement can be set with an expiry date; after that date it stops appearing in the app automatically. There is no length limit, but shorter, direct messages are more likely to be read. Members are sent a push notification when a new announcement is published.

Reports

Reports (Reports in the sidebar) gives admin and instructors a view of the club's flying and financial activity for the current financial year. All figures are scoped to the FY configured in Settings → Membership. Available views:

  • Dashboard KPIs — at the top: total flight hours, number of flights (solo vs dual), revenue charged, revenue collected, active member count, and total outstanding account debt. A quick pulse check for the secretary or CFI at the start of each week.
  • Hours by aircraft — a bar chart showing how many hours each aircraft flew each month of the FY. Filter by owned fleet, leased fleet, or all. Useful for seeing which aircraft are being fully utilised and which are underused.
  • Hours by instructor — a chart showing dual hours per instructor per month. Helps the CFI track each instructor's contribution and workload across the year.
  • Member activity — a table listing every member who flew during the FY, with their total flight count and total hours, sorted by highest hours. Useful for identifying the club's most active flyers and those who haven't flown recently.
  • Payment summary — a month-by-month table of flights completed, total charged, and total collected. The difference (charged minus collected) is outstanding for that month. Useful for the treasurer to track how well charges are being collected and where arrears are building.
  • Pivot table (Reports → Pivot) — a fully filterable flight log. Filter by date range, aircraft, member, flight type, instructor; sort and group as needed. Useful for ad-hoc analysis: "how many hours did ZK-ABC fly in July with training flights?" The pivot exports are available via the Data export page for spreadsheet analysis.
  • Members export — download a spreadsheet of all member data including standing, role, subscription expiry, account balance, and contact details. Used by the secretary for AGM preparation or mail-merges.
  • AI Ask (where enabled) — a natural language query interface over your club's flight and member data. Ask questions like "how many hours did we fly last month?" or "which members haven't flown in six months?" Useful for committee reporting without needing to know SQL or the pivot filter syntax.

Reports are read-only — no records are changed. Quality depends on completeness of flight completions, charge records, and maintenance entries. If flight completions are missing meter readings or charges, the revenue and hours figures will be understated.

Safety events (occurrences)

Safety → Events is the occurrence reporting system. Any member or staff can submit a report covering anything from a minor snag to a significant near-miss. Reports are categorised by the event types defined in Settings → Types → Event types.

Submitting a report. From Safety → Events → Submit, the reporter selects an event type, describes what happened (what was observed, what conditions applied, what action was taken), and optionally links it to a specific aircraft or booking. Reports can be submitted anonymously if the club's settings allow it.

Review and action. Submitted reports go to a review queue. Admin (or anyone with Safety access) can open a report, add observations, assign action items to named people with due dates, and mark the report as resolved once all items are closed. Action items that are overdue or approaching their due date surface in the sidebar Attention badge — so open items don't get forgotten between committee meetings.

Safety → Action items. In addition to viewing actions within each report, the Action items page shows all open action items across every report in one consolidated list. This is the view to use at the start of a safety review or committee meeting — it shows what's outstanding, who it's assigned to, and when it's due, without having to open each report individually.

Export. The occurrence list can be exported (Safety → Events → Export) for committee review, minutes attachment, or CAA submission. The export includes event type, date, description, status, and action summary.

Link to maintenance. Aircraft snags logged at flight completion are linked to the occurrence system and appear in the aircraft's maintenance history — creating a traceable chain from snag report → action item → maintenance log entry → resolution.

Data export (Settings → Data)

The Data page (Settings → Data, admin only) provides bulk exports of your club's data as downloadable CSV files. Available datasets:

  • Members — name, email, role, standing, CAA number, phone, account balance, subscription expiry
  • Flight history — all completed flights: date, aircraft, pilot, instructor, flight type, hours, charges, payment method
  • Aircraft — fleet list with current status, maintenance items, and maintenance log entries
  • Financial — account transactions, invoice payments, outstanding balances

Use these exports for long-term archiving, importing into Xero or your accounting software, or when your committee wants a full data extract for auditing purposes. The data page also provides an import template — a pre-formatted CSV you can fill in and use for bulk-loading aircraft records or member records when setting up the club for the first time.

Integrity (Manage → Integrity)

The Integrity page (Manage → Integrity, admin only) cross-checks the database for internal consistency across three areas. If the sidebar shows a red badge on Integrity, there are issues requiring attention. The page does not change any data unless you explicitly use a correction action.

Financial tab

Runs four checks across your financial records:

  • Account balance drift — compares each member's stored account balance against the sum of their individual ledger transactions. A mismatch means the displayed balance is out of sync with the actual transaction history. To fix: use the Fix balance drift button that appears when issues are found — it recomputes every balance from the transaction ledger and saves the corrected values. The transaction ledger is always the source of truth. Run this if a member reports their balance looks incorrect.
  • Flight charge drift — checks that the total stored on each flight completion matches the sum of its individual charge line items. A mismatch typically results from a line item being added or removed without the total being recalculated. To fix: open the flagged flight completion (use the View link), review the charge items, and re-apply the charges. If a line item was incorrectly added or deleted, correct it and save.
  • Flight payment drift — checks that the amount-paid figure on each flight completion matches the sum of its recorded payments. A mismatch can occur if a payment record was edited outside the normal workflow. To fix: open the flagged flight completion, review the payment records, and correct or remove any erroneous entries.
  • Invoice/FC payment sync — identifies invoices marked as paid where the linked flight completion still shows a balance owing. To fix: open the invoice and confirm the payment was applied correctly; if it was, re-mark the flight completion as paid from the booking detail page.

Operations tab

Checks for meter reading inconsistencies on both Hobbs and Tacho between consecutive flights on each aircraft. Two types of issue are reported:

  • Gap — the next flight's start reading is higher than the previous flight's end reading. Unaccounted time has elapsed between flights (ground run, ferry, maintenance run).
  • Overlap — the next flight's start reading is lower than the previous flight's end reading. The readings contradict each other — one was entered incorrectly.

To fix either: click View log → next to the flagged entry to open the full maintenance log for that aircraft. Find the relevant entry and click the pencil icon on that row to open the amend dialog. Correct the start and/or end reading, enter a reason, and save. The Integrity page will reflect the correction immediately. If the gap represents genuinely unaccounted time (e.g. a ground run by a third party), the readings are correct and no action is needed — the gap will remain visible as a record of the unaccounted time. Unresolved genuine overlaps mean one reading is wrong and maintenance hours are being mis-stated.

Members tab

Identifies members whose standing is still Active but whose subscription has expired. This can happen if a member's expiry date was updated manually without triggering the standing check, or if the auto-lapse has not been run since the expiry date passed.

To fix: for each flagged member, either run Run auto-lapse now (Settings → Membership) to process all overdue members in one go, or update an individual member's standing manually from their membership tab (Members → [member] → Membership → Edit standing). Check this page before bulk renewal season to ensure the member list is accurate before generating invoices.

Run the Integrity check before committee meetings, before any bulk renewal action, and any time a member reports that their balance or flight record looks wrong.
