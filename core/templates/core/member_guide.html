MEMBER HELP GUIDE

Member help guide

Welcome to {{ club.name }} on ClubHangar — the system the club uses to manage bookings, your membership, and your flying account. This short guide covers what you, as a member, need to know. Most day-to-day tasks (booking a flight, checking your balance) are quickest on the mobile app, but everything is available here on the web too.

Welcome

ClubHangar is where you book aircraft and instructors, keep your pilot details up to date, and see what you owe or have paid. The club's office staff and instructors use the same system to run bookings, invoicing, and aircraft maintenance — so when you book or update your details, they see it straight away.

Signing in

You sign in with your email address and the password you chose when you accepted your invitation.

  • Forgot your password? Click Forgot password? on the sign-in screen and we'll email you a link to set a new one.
  • Belong to more than one club? After signing in you'll be asked to pick which club you want to open. You can switch clubs any time from the menu — you don't need to sign in again.

The mobile app

For everyday use — booking, checking your balance, viewing the schedule — the mobile app is the easiest way in. It's the same account, designed for your phone.

  1. On your phone, open {{ request.scheme }}://{{ request.get_host }}/app/{{ club.slug }}/
  2. Sign in with your email and password.
  3. Add it to your home screen (your browser's Share → Add to Home Screen) so it opens like a normal app.
Tip: Turn on notifications in the app (Profile → Notifications) so you're told when a booking is confirmed or an invoice is issued.

There's a separate, even shorter guide for the mobile app — find it inside the app under Profile.

The calendar

The calendar shows every aircraft and instructor across the day, so you can see at a glance what's free and what's already booked. Your own bookings are highlighted. Use it to plan when to fly before you request a booking.

Booking a flight

→
2Staff confirm it
→
3Fly
→
4Charged to your account

1 — Request: Pick the aircraft (and instructor if you need one), choose a date and time, and send the request. The quickest way is the mobile app's Book button, which only offers slots that are actually free.

2 — Confirmation: A booking starts as Pending until the club confirms it. You'll be notified when it's confirmed.

3 — On the day: An instructor or duty member checks you out before the flight and checks you back in afterwards, recording the hours flown.

4 — Charging: The flight's cost is worked out from the hours and the club's rates, and applied to your account automatically.

Why might I not be able to book? Bookings need your membership to be active and your pilot credentials (medical, flight review, ratings) to be current. If something has lapsed, the system will let the club know — keep your details up to date (see Profile & credentials below).

Your membership

Your membership has a standing that controls whether you can book:

  • Pending — you've joined but the committee hasn't yet ratified your membership, or your first subscription isn't paid. You can look around but not book.
  • Active — you're a paid-up member and can book flights.

When your annual subscription is due, the club issues you a renewal invoice. Once it's paid, your membership stays active for the new year.

Account & payments

Your account is your running balance with the club. Flights and any other charges are applied to it; payments and top-ups credit it. You can see your balance and history at any time (it's on the Account tab in the mobile app).

When the club issues you an invoice — for a subscription or other charges — you'll be notified. How you pay (EFTPOS, cash, or bank transfer) depends on your club's normal arrangements; the office marks it paid once received.

Profile & credentials

Keeping your details current matters — your credentials (medical certificate, flight review/BFR, ratings) determine what you're allowed to book and fly.

  • Update your contact details and emergency contact under Profile.
  • Add or update a credential (e.g. a renewed medical) so the system knows it's current — in the mobile app, Profile → Add credential.
Heads up: If a credential lapses, you may be blocked from booking or checking out a flight until it's renewed and recorded. Update it as soon as you have the new expiry date.

Getting help

If something doesn't look right — a charge you don't recognise, a booking that didn't confirm, or trouble signing in — contact the club's office or your usual instructor. They can see your account and bookings and sort most things out quickly.
